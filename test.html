I'm not going to say not to take this class, as its a requirement for pretty much everything CS related. But unless you are well versed in python, prepare to dedicate a hefty amount of time to this course. Most of the assignments take some arbitrary concept, and apply it to computer science in the form of the homeworks. On paper, it sounds good that they give you a varied background in different computer science niches. However, most of them involve a poor explanation of a complex topic (like projective geometry)in the form of video lectures and then an application of the principles that is usually convoluted and unnecessarily difficult to understand. The most unnecessary part of this course though are the recipes. There is no consistency on what makes a good recipe. They give a recipe guide on canvas, but it's incredibly vague to the point that you must to go to at least 2 TAs in order to get a good grade on it. The problem is that every TA has a different idea of what a good recipe is. What one TA says is ok might be something that another TA takes off a lot of credit for. Since everyone needs to go to a TA, other students and I would often have to wait in hour long queues in order to have a TA check my recipe. With that being said, the TAs were often helpful and friendly when you needed help, and usually did their best to help students. Overall, if you're experienced in python this class is probably more tedious if anything, however it is very difficult (albeit doable) otherwise. If this isn't a major req for you, I would avoid this course.
